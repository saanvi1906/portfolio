BrainDump.AI - AI Stress to Success Hub

Problem: Students constantly deal with exams, deadlines, internship pressure, and personal stress, but most tools either store notes passively or fail to give immediate, actionable support.

What I Built: BrainDump.AI is an AI-powered web app that transforms typed or spoken stress dumps into four useful outputs: an action plan, a wellness reset tip, a motivation boost, and a stress score from 0.0–1.0.

Key Features: The MVP supports typed and voice-based input, returns structured AI outputs designed for frontend display, and includes a mood dashboard that visualizes stress levels over time using color-coded blobs from green to red.

Memory Integration: Using Supermemory, each stress dump and AI response is stored and retrieved across sessions so the system can identify recurring stress patterns and provide more personalized, trend-aware suggestions over time.

Example Output: For a student input like “I’m drowning in assignments and don’t know where to start,” the app can return 2–4 concrete next steps, a one-line wellness reset, an encouraging reframe, and a high stress score such as 0.83.

Why It Stood Out: This project combined productivity, wellness, memory, and AI into one product experience, turning emotional overload into clarity and immediate action instead of just storing text.

Technologies: JavaScript, AI APIs, Prompt Engineering, Voice Input, Supermemory

Tracking Global Renewable Energy Growth

Problem: Global energy datasets are large and difficult to interpret, making it hard to compare renewable energy adoption across countries, time periods, and energy sources.

What I Built: I helped build an interactive data visualization platform exploring renewable energy growth across 200+ countries from 1990 to 2023 using real-world energy data.

Visualizations Implemented: The platform includes a choropleth world map, a multi-country line chart, a stacked area chart, a diverging bar chart, a scatter plot, and radial flower glyphs to analyze energy composition and adoption trends.

Why It Matters: The project makes long-term renewable energy trends easier to understand by turning raw energy data into interactive visual stories that support comparison, exploration, and analysis.

Technologies: D3.js, JavaScript, TopoJSON, CSS, Data Visualization
